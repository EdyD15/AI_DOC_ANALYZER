# Product

## Register

product

## Users

General knowledge workers — professionals, students, and everyday users who need to extract information from documents. Their job when they open the app: upload a document and get precise answers fast. They may be on a deadline, in research mode, or trying to avoid rereading the same 40-page PDF for the third time.

## Product Purpose

DocuMind AI is a document intelligence tool. Users upload PDFs, Word docs, text files, and images, then have a focused conversation with the content. Success looks like: the user gets a reliable, specific answer in one or two questions instead of skimming for 20 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Precise, capable, focused.

Voice: Direct and confident — like a smart colleague who actually read the whole document. No hedging, no filler. Answers the question.

Emotional goal: The user should feel like the tool is *with* them — attentive and ready. Not like they are fighting the interface to get to the answer.

## Anti-references

- **Cyberpunk / neon / dark gaming aesthetics**: glowing borders, neon accents, dark-mode-as-gimmick. DocuMind's power is in its output, not visual theater.
- **Enterprise corporate**: navy + grey palette, dense data tables, the visual language of a 2015 CRM. DocuMind serves individuals, not IT departments.

## Design Principles

1. **The conversation is the product.** The chat area is the primary surface. Sidebar, controls, and upload are secondary scaffolding — they should not compete for attention.
2. **Precision over personality.** Type, spacing, and contrast carry more weight than color or decorative elements. Every element justifies itself.
3. **Don't announce capability, demonstrate it.** No hero copy, no feature callouts inside the app. Users learn capability from using it.
4. **Quiet confidence.** The interface does not shout. Good tools feel inevitable, not impressive.
5. **Speed over ceremony.** Upload → ask → answer. No loading spectacle, no onboarding gates, no friction theater.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Standard contrast ratios, keyboard navigation. No reliance on color alone to convey meaning. Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
